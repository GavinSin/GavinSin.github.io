How PruRisk Works

The objective of a risk questionnaire is to identified common risks that a business may have been overlooked and provide suggested solutions.

To access and evaluate the SME business risk, a questionnaire, including 20 questions, was developed. Several questions collected general information about how an SME business managed and dealt with common types of business risk faced by its specific industry. Sample of a risk preparedness question is:

How prepared you are with the business continuity plan (BCP) ?

Question and the Points

A question can comes in the form of two or four options. Ranging from the minimum of 0 point to maximum of 3 points. The purpose of the question is to identify preparedness of a particular risk.

3 points

If either option (c) or (d) is chosen, it shows that a business has prepared sufficiently enough of a particular risk. However, some risks required two or more questions. The following shows how it is being evaluated:

Cyberattack risk:
       Qn 1: 2 points out of a maximum of 3 points
       Qn 2: 3 points out of a maximum of 3 points

The preparedness of cyberattack risk
= Total points selected / Total points of Cyberattack risk
= ( 2 + 3 ) points / ( 3 + 3 ) points
= 2.5 points >= 2 points (Prepared quite okay or more)

When the 20qns questionnaire is finished, we will have a list of risks that a business didn't prepare or managed well in. With that many risks, it is important to sort with priorities and shows first the top 3 highest risks faced by the business.
